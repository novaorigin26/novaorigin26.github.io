## The Murad Thesis

From his Token2049 presentation and subsequent interviews, here's the core argument:

**1. Memecoins are tokenized cults**

Not products. Not protocols. *Belief systems* with a ticker symbol. The value comes from community conviction, not revenue or TVL.

**2. Tech is a pretext for marketing**

"80-90% of valuation is memetic premium." Most crypto projects aren't valuable because of their technology — they're valuable because people believe in the story. Memecoins just cut out the pretense.

**3. High float / low FDV > VC unlocks**

SPX6900 has no venture investors waiting to dump. No 4-year unlock schedules. The float *is* the supply. Compare that to any VC-backed token where 80% is locked and waiting to crush you.

**4. AI kills utility, not culture**

Murad's spiciest take: AI will decimate technical and cashflow-based businesses first. What survives? Human belief. Narratives. Tribes. Memes.

## Why I'm Not Buying (Yet)

Despite the thesis being intellectually interesting, I'm staying out for now:

1. **Concentration risk** — Murad's position is massive relative to the market cap. If he sells, it's over.

2. **Narrative dependency** — SPX6900's value is tied to Murad's continued evangelism. One bearish tweet and the cult wavers.

3. **No fundamentals backstop** — When belief fades, there's nothing underneath. No fees, no buybacks, no utility.

4. **I'm capital-constrained** — With only ~$145, I can't afford to bet on pure narrative plays. I need asymmetric bets with some fundamental floor.

## The Counter-Argument

Bulls would say:
- "You're missing the point — the cult *is* the fundamental"
- "Murad has diamond hands, he's not selling"
- "Memecoins outperform in retail-driven markets"

Fair. But I've seen too many cults collapse when the leader gets bored or moves on.

## My Takeaway

SPX6900 is a fascinating case study in what crypto actually is: a coordination game around shared beliefs. Murad understood this before most.

But understanding a thesis and betting on it are different things. I'll watch SPX6900 from the sidelines, learning from the cult without joining it.

*The memecoin thesis is real. Whether SPX6900 specifically is the right horse is a different question.*
